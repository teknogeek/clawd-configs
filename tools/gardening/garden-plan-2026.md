## Raised Bed Assignments (4×2ft each)

| Bed | Crops | Notes |
|-----|-------|-------|
| **Bed 1** | Lettuce + Spinach | Cool crops, harvest early spring; replant with cilantro/basil after |
| **Bed 2** | Broccoli + Kale | Brassicas together; 2-3 plants each |
| **Bed 3** | Chili Peppers + Basil | Full sun lovers; don't plant basil out until May 11+ |
| **Bed 4** | Green Onions + Carrots | Dense planting; both shallow enough for 12" bed depth |

## In-Ground Plot (~15×20ft = 300 sqft)

| Zone | Size | Crop | Notes |
|------|------|------|-------|
| Row A | 4×10ft | Potatoes | Plant sets 4" deep, mound soil as they grow |
| Row B | 2×10ft | Onions | Plant sets or seeds alongside potatoes |
| Block C | 4×8ft | Sweet Corn | Plant in grid (4×4 min) for pollination; not rows |
| Block D | 4×8ft | Sweet Potatoes | Vines spread wide; give them room |

**Placement rules:**
- Put corn on the NORTH side of the plot so it doesn't shade other crops
- Sweet potatoes can go south side, they don't mind being shaded slightly
- Keep 10–15ft buffer from treeline to avoid root competition

---

## Siting & Prep

- **Treeline buffer:** 10–15ft of grass between woods and any garden zones
- **Prep in-ground plot now (Mar 13–30):**
  - Option A (no-dig): Lay cardboard over grass, top with 4–6" compost/topsoil, let sit 2–3 weeks
  - Option B: Till grass under, add compost, rake level
- **Raised beds:** already placed; amend soil with compost if needed before planting
